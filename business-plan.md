# Nestful — Business Plan (Working Draft v0.1)

**Domain:** nestful.com *(not yet acquired — see Next Steps)*
**One-liner:** The dating app where openness to kids and dependents is settled before the first message — not on date three.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Nestful is a dating platform built on a single core principle: **pre-screened, judgment-free openness.** Every member declares — before matching — whether they have children or significant dependents, and whether they're open to dating someone who does. The awkward, relationship-derailing conversation that usually surfaces on date two or three is resolved on day zero.

The result: parents and caregivers date without fear of the "reveal," and singles who are genuinely open to joining a family find each other efficiently. Nobody wastes emotional investment on a fundamental incompatibility.

---

## 2. The Problem

- On mainstream apps (Hinge, Bumble, Tinder), the kids question is a skippable profile field, often left blank or buried. It carries stigma, so many parents delay disclosing.
- The conversation typically happens **after** chemistry and emotional attachment have formed — by date two or three. When it goes badly, both people feel misled or rejected, and the parent internalizes the stigma.
- Non-parents who *are* open to dating parents have no efficient way to signal it, so parents assume the worst and self-filter.
- Caregivers of **extended dependents** (an elderly parent at home, a disabled sibling, guardianship of a niece or nephew) face the same dynamic with even less vocabulary for it — no app addresses them at all.

## 3. The Solution — The Core Principle

**Mutual openness is the entry ticket, not a filter you hope people used honestly.**

1. At signup, every member completes a short, warm "Nest Profile": do you have kids or dependents (no details required), and what are you open to in a partner?
2. Matching only surfaces mutually compatible openness. If you're not open to dating someone with young children, you simply never see each other. No rejection event ever occurs.
3. Because *everyone* on the app opted into this premise, the culture is judgment-free by construction. Being a parent on Nestful isn't a disclosure — it's the point.

---

## 4. Community Language — Naming the Two Groups

Principles: name people by what they **bring or seek**, never by what they lack. Avoid "single parents vs. singles" (parents are single too), avoid "childless/childfree" (both are politically loaded), and never anything adjacent to "baggage" or "package deal."

**Recommended concept — everyone's a Nester, badges differ:**

| Group | Badge name | Framing |
|---|---|---|
| Members with kids / dependents | **Full Nest** 🪺 | "My nest has company — kids, or someone I care for." |
| Members without dependents, open to them | **Nest-Ready** 🌿 | "My nest has room — I'm open to joining or building a family." |

Both labels are aspirational and symmetric: one describes a nest that's full, the other a nest with room. Neither is a deficit.

**Alternate concepts (backup options):**
- **Nesters & Open Nests** — same metaphor, softer.
- **Rooted & Ready** — "Rooted" (established, grounded, comes with people who matter) and "Ready" (prepared to grow roots together).
- **"I come with a crew" / "Ready to join a crew"** — playful, profile-prompt style rather than a formal label; could work as onboarding copy even if badges use the Nest language.

**Depth-of-openness (product gold, not just labels):** openness isn't binary. The Nest Profile should capture a spectrum on both sides:
- Full Nest side: ages/stage (young kids, teens, adult dependents, caregiving for a parent), custody/time rhythm (every day, alternating weeks), and how soon a partner would meet the family.
- Nest-Ready side: open to young kids vs. teens vs. adult dependents; wanting a hands-on family role vs. supportive-partner role; open to having more children together or not.

This turns the single awkward question into a rich matching dimension no competitor has.

## 5. Target Market

**Primary segments:**
1. Single parents, ages ~25–50, tired of the disclosure dance on mainstream apps.
2. Non-parents genuinely open to (or actively seeking) a partner with a family — currently invisible to each other and to parents.
3. **Caregivers of extended dependents** — the underserved differentiator. Roughly 50+ million Americans provide unpaid care to an adult family member (AARP/NAC estimates); many are single and dating with the same undisclosed-obligation anxiety parents feel.

**Market size signals (verify during research phase):**
- ~11 million single-parent households in the U.S. (Census Bureau).
- Online dating is the #1 way U.S. couples now meet; niche vertical apps (Stir, Heybaby, religious/community apps) have proven the "shared premise" model.

## 6. Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning | Gap Nestful exploits |
|---|---|---|
| **Stir** (Match Group, 2022) | Dating for single parents | Parent-to-parent skew; doesn't cultivate the open non-parent pool; no dependents beyond kids |
| **Heybaby** | Family-minded dating (have kids or want kids) | Future-kids focus; screening is shallow |
| **SingleParentMeet** etc. | Legacy niche sites | Dated product, parent-only pond |
| **Hinge/Bumble filters** | "Have kids / want kids" fields | Optional, skippable, no mutual pre-screening, stigma persists |

**Nestful's differentiators:**
1. Mutual pre-screening as the *architecture*, not a filter — mismatches never see each other.
2. Both populations recruited deliberately: parents AND the open singles who want to find them.
3. Extended dependents / caregivers included — a first in the category.
4. Depth-of-openness matching (Section 4) rather than a yes/no checkbox.

## 7. Product — MVP Feature Set

**MVP (Phase 1):**
- Nest Profile onboarding (the pre-screen) — warm, ~2 minutes, judgment-free copy throughout
- Inclusive identity & preferences: gender as Woman / Man / Nonbinary plus an optional
  free-text "in my own words" (never an "Other" bucket); optional pronouns;
  "Show me" as a **multi-select** (Women / Men / Nonbinary people, with an Everyone
  shortcut) — a binary "both" erases nonbinary members. Matching is mutual: you only
  see people whose preferences include you, consistent with the pre-screen ethos
- Optional household size as **ranges, not exact counts** (1 · 2 · 3+ per kid category,
  shown as "2 young kids") — matches want the household shape, but exact counts + ages +
  city over-identifies real children; required fields stay limited to the nest "shape."
  No counts for caregiving ("caring for a parent" needs no number)
- **"Nest-Ready" as an exclusive openness option:** Full Nest members can explicitly
  opt into partners without kids/dependents; Nest-Ready members can never select it —
  Nest-Ready ↔ Nest-Ready pairing is structurally impossible (it would defeat the
  app's purpose). Matching honors this mutually: a Nest-Ready member only sees Full
  Nest members who opted in
- **Kid-count preference & filtering:** counts appear as a search filter (1 / 2 / 3+),
  and each member can set a private "how many kids am I open to" preference that
  silently shapes their deck — a personal filter, never shown to other members
- Mutual-openness matching engine (hard gate) + standard swipe/like/message flow
- Full Nest / Nest-Ready badges and depth-of-openness display
- Photo + ID verification (trust is the brand)
- Conversation starters that normalize family talk ("My weekends usually look like…")

**Phase 2:**
- Scheduling-aware features (parents' availability windows; "free this Saturday" status)
- Community layer: forums/events for single parents and caregivers
- Optional background-check integration
- Video "coffee chat" (low-babysitter-cost first dates)

**Explicitly NOT in product:** photos of children on profiles (policy decision — see Safety), children's names/details in structured data.

## 8. Trust & Safety (non-negotiable, brand-defining)

- **Children's privacy first:** no minors in profile photos (or blurred-by-default), no children's names or schools in structured fields, aggressive moderation of the same.
- ID verification at signup; liveness check on photos.
- Anyone screening for "access to children" signals gets escalated review — partner with safety orgs; this category attracts bad actors and Nestful must be visibly hardened against them.
- Block/report with fast human review; safety center content for single parents (meeting logistics, when to introduce kids).
- COPPA doesn't apply directly (users are 18+) but children's-data hygiene is still a legal and reputational requirement.

## 9. Business Model — Freemium + Nestful+ Upgrade Funnel

### Launch phasing (decided July 2026)

**Phase A — Freemium BETA (now).** No payment gates anywhere. Free for everyone:
the pre-screened deck, **Nest filters**, **Rhythm Match**, and **see who liked you**.
Strict daily caps for everyone — **8 likes and 2 notes per day** — kept deliberately:
they make the app feel exclusive and purposeful, not crippled. Hitting a cap shows
"Purposeful, on purpose" messaging plus a **Nestful+ waitlist** signup (demand
measurement before payments exist). The entire upgrade funnel sits behind a feature
flag (`FEATURES.upgradeEnabled = false` in the app).

**Phase B — Nestful+ launch.** Prerequisites: LLC formed, business bank account,
payment processor (Stripe for web; App Store/Play billing for native). Flip the flag
and move to the paid tier: unlimited likes, Private Nest mode, weekly Boost,
10 notes/week. Waitlist members get launch pricing.

**Phase C — Partnerships.** Date-night perks (sitter & venue deals) ship only after
partner agreements are signed.

**Platform path:** JavaScript web app (PWA, installable on phones) first → native
iOS/Android via a wrapper (e.g., Capacitor) reusing the same codebase later.

**Governing principle: never paywall the pre-screen or safety.** Mutual openness
screening is the brand promise; gating it would recreate the exact awkwardness the
app exists to eliminate. Monetize velocity, visibility, and convenience.

**What incumbents monetize (for reference):** Hinge caps free likes (~8/day) and sells
scarce "roses" (super-likes with notes, 1 free/week); Tinder/Bumble sell
see-who-liked-you, unlimited swipes, boosts, filters, incognito, travel mode. The proven
levers: attention scarcity, information asymmetry, visibility.

### Free tier (must prove the core promise)
- Full pre-screened deck (never limited or degraded)
- ~8 likes/day
- 2 personalized notes/week
- Standard visibility

### Nestful+ (subscription, ~$19.99–24.99/mo, cheaper 3/6-mo commitments)
- Unlimited likes; see who liked you
- 10 notes/week — **notes stay capped even for premium**: their value is scarcity;
  unlimited notes would turn the app's highest-signal gesture into spam
- **Nest filters** — depth-of-openness filtering (ages, time rhythm, caregiving type)
- **Rhythm Match** — custody-schedule compatibility ("their kid-free weekends align
  with yours"); high practical value, no competitor offers it
- **Private Nest mode** — profile visible only to people you've liked; premium
  incognito matters uniquely to parents (school parents, coworkers, exes)
- Weekly profile Boost included
- **Date-night perks** — partner sitter discounts / family-friendly venue deals
  (structurally impossible for mainstream apps to copy credibly)

### Consumables
- **Golden Egg 🥚** — a premium note that lands at the top of the recipient's queue
  (the Hinge-rose analog, on-brand)
- Extra Boosts

### Funnel triggers (fire at moments of felt scarcity, never mid-safety-flow)
- Hitting the daily like cap; composing a note beyond the weekly allowance
- "4 people liked you this week" with blurred avatars
- Rhythm teaser: "3 matches share your custody rhythm — see them with Nestful+"
- Discounted first month; consider 1-week premium trial at signup

**Avoid:** selling data (brand poison for this audience), ad-heavy free tier, and any
upgrade prompt attached to safety features or the pre-screen itself.

## 10. Go-to-Market

- **Beachhead:** one or two metro areas to solve liquidity (dating apps die from empty match pools). Seed both populations deliberately — parent groups AND "open to dating a parent" messaging.
- Channels: single-parent communities (Facebook groups, subreddits, PTA-adjacent networks), caregiver communities (AARP-adjacent, support forums), TikTok/IG creators who are dating single parents ("I married a single mom" content performs).
- PR hook: "the app that kills the date-three kids talk."
- Brand voice: warm, funny, zero pity. The enemy is awkwardness, not singleness.

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

- **Liquidity / cold start** — the classic dating-app killer. Mitigate with geographic focus and dual-sided seeding.
- **Population imbalance** (too many Full Nest, too few Nest-Ready). Mitigate with targeted Nest-Ready marketing; monitor ratio as a core KPI.
- **Match Group competition** (Stir could copy features). Mitigate with the caregiver segment, community depth, and brand authenticity.
- **Safety incident involving a child** — existential reputational risk. Mitigate with Section 8 investments from day one, not later.
- **Domain unavailable/expensive** — have backups: getnestful.com, nestful.app, nestful.co.

## 13. Legal & Entity Protection (added July 2026)

- **Entity:** form **Nestful, LLC** before public launch; all terms, hosting, and
  payment accounts go under the LLC, shielding personal assets.
- **Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy drafts** live in `legal/` (and ship in-app
  at `terms.html` / `privacy.html`, linked from a **required signup consent checkbox**
  with an acceptance timestamp stored per account). Key protections drafted:
  - **UGC:** users own their content but license it to the company; users are solely
    responsible for it; Section 230 platform (not publisher) positioning; users
    warrant they hold rights to what they upload
  - **Child-safety policy** as a binding conduct rule (no minors in photos, ever)
  - 18+ eligibility, no-background-check disclaimer, meet-at-your-own-risk warning
  - Limitation of liability + indemnification by users; DMCA process; arbitration
- **Required before launch:** attorney review of both documents (they are drafts,
  not legal advice), DMCA agent registration with the U.S. Copyright Office, and
  state-specific auto-renewal disclosures before Nestful+ payments begin.

## 14. Hosting & User Management (future-proofed path)

| Stage | Hosting | Accounts / signup tracking |
|---|---|---|
| **Beta (now)** | Static app; free host (Netlify/Cloudflare Pages). `?sandbox` URL gives an isolated test environment; production data untouched | Browser localStorage; founder dashboard at `#admin` with signup counts, waitlist, CSV export |
| **Hosted beta** | Same host; `nestful.com` on production branch, staging on a password-protected branch deploy (Netlify gives both free) | **Supabase free tier**: real auth + Postgres; signups visible in Supabase dashboard; admin panel reads the same tables |
| **Scale** | Same static front end + Supabase paid tier; add analytics (privacy-respecting, e.g., Plausible) | Row-level security, email verification, moderation queue |

Chosen because each step reuses the previous one — the front end never needs a
rewrite, and Supabase's auth/Postgres carry from free tier to scale.

**Should the database sync to a third-party vendor for BETA?** Yes — recommended
before real users sign up. localStorage-only has no cross-device access, no
encryption-at-rest guarantee, can't power real email, and can't fulfill data-subject
requests (deletion/export) that CCPA/GDPR require. **Supabase** (Postgres + Auth +
Row Level Security, free tier, region choice) is the recommended vendor: it's the
natural next step from the current schema, needs a Data Processing Agreement, and
should be added to the Privacy Policy's vendor list once selected. A transactional
email provider (Resend, Postmark, or SendGrid) is a second "vendor" needed at the
same time — the app's email-sending code (`sendEmail()`) is already written to swap
into whichever one is chosen; see the Founder Dashboard note below.

**Backend scaffold (built July 2026):** `backend/schema.sql`, `backend/supabase-client.js`,
and `backend/SETUP.md` are ready — Postgres schema with Row Level Security for
profiles/likes/usage caps, a client wrapper mirroring the app's current functions,
and a click-by-click guide covering Supabase project setup, the Brevo email vendor
(chosen for its free-tier automation + built-in CRM — closest match to "one unified
comms tool"), domain (nestfulapp.com) sender verification, and connecting Brevo's
SMTP to Supabase's password-reset emails. The live app still runs on localStorage
until the wiring step in SETUP.md Phase 3 is done together.

**Founder dashboard (built July 2026):** `#admin` now has two tabs — **Users**
(search by name/email, click a row for account details and actions: resend welcome
email, send password reset, delete account) and **Outbox** (every "sent" email,
reviewable before a real ESP is wired up). Welcome emails fire automatically on
signup; password reset uses a 1-hour expiring token via a `?reset=` link, matching
the flow a real ESP will send. Self-service password change lives in Edit profile.

**⚠️ Naming collision found:** `nestful.app` is an active, unrelated productivity
app ("Nestful — Manage yourself with spontaneous productivity," blog at
blog.nestful.app). Separately, **Nestfully, LLC** holds live USPTO trademarks for
"NESTFULLY" and "NESTAVA" (real estate consumer software, filed 2022 and 2024) —
phonetically close to Nestful. Neither is in the dating category, which reduces
(but doesn't eliminate) conflict risk. **Run a formal trademark clearance search
in the dating/social-services class before finalizing the brand name** — this is
now a higher-priority step than it was before this research.

**Cost-effective registrars (2026 pricing found in research):** Porkbun (flat
~$11/yr, no renewal markup, free WHOIS privacy + SSL) and Cloudflare Registrar
(at-cost, ~$10.44/yr, no markup) were the best value; Namecheap is a common
middle-ground choice. Avoid registrars with a cheap first year and a much higher
renewal price — compare the 5-year total cost, not the intro price.
